What types of neighborhoods are most affected by “business-like” Airbnb properties?

                        By facetting all the previous measures and mapping them geographically, we can generate 
                a visualization of the properties that pose a risk to the housing market and where they 
                are. After concluding where the majority of such properties are clustered, we can begin 
                to analyze similarities among the neighborhoods with a high density of risk-posing 
                properties.
                        This map shows the location of all Airbnb listings that are renting out an entire house 
                and are available for 95 days or more out of the year. Previous research conducted has 
                shown that landlords are preferring to rent out properties via Airbnb instead of to 
                long-term residents, thereby decreasing the local housing stock, negatively impacting 
                affordable housing, and increasing the average cost of rent in areas. Neighborhoods like 
                Downtown LA, Hollywood, and Santa Monica, which are already tourist-heavy areas with an 
                expensive cost of living to begin with, have an especially abundant amount of such 
                listings. Additionally, tourist-heavy areas tend to generate more work opportunities, 
                meaning low-income workers are even more affected as they are forced to commute long 
                distances as a result of being unable to afford to live in the area. These factors 
                combined indicate that Airbnb is directly negatively impacting the local residents of 
                Los Angeles, especially those who cannot or are hardly affording to live in the city. 
                When toggling the map to turn off the filter of availability, there is a small difference 
                between the two, indicating that not many homes in LA are being regularly rented out. This 
                shows that the majority of such listings are highly available and therefore taking away 
                from the housing stock in Los Angeles.
