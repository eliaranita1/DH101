Distribution of properties by neighborhood 



                        This map of Los Angeles, California displays the spatial distribution of Airbnb listings, 
                based on the publicly available Airbnb dataset. The densest areas include the coastal 
                municipalities like Santa Monica and Long Beach, as well as Downtown Los Angeles, Beverly 
                Hills, and Hollywood. The outer suburban areas like Compton and Lakewood have the smallest 
                density of listings. This is to be expected, since travelers are more likely to stay close to 
                tourist attractions in the downtown and coastal areas of Los Angeles. 
                        While Airbnb now seems like a ubiquitous option for lodging to tourists, local residents 
                may not be aware of the scope of Airbnb in their area and how it could be affecting their 
                neighborhoods. This initial visualization is a baseline representation of the areas in 
                which there is a strong presence of Airbnb properties. The map includes all listings 
                regardless of review volume, meaning that the density of the red dots speaks to the supply 
                of Airbnb listings by area, while review data can indicate the demand. The data 
                visualization highlights the spatial concentration of revenue for Airbnb hosts, which would 
                generally be highest in the most dense areas on the map with the most listings. Our other 
                visualizations involving listing types and prices can further enhance this narrative, 
                breaking down the distribution of revenue and what groups are benefitting most.
